A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India

Breif Timeline of Dr.A.P.J Abdul Kalam:

  • October 15, 1931: Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam is born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • 1954: Kalam graduates in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli.
  • 1955: He joins the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT) to study aerospace engineering.
  • 1960: Kalam completes his engineering degree and joins the Aeronautical Development Establishment (ADE) as a scientist.
  • 1969: He moves to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and plays a crucial role in the development of India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle, SLV-III.
  • 1980: Kalam becomes the project director of India's ambitious integrated guided missile development program.
  • 1992: He becomes the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister of India and the Secretary of the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO).
  • 1998: Kalam plays a pivotal role in the successful test firing of the Agni-II, a medium-range ballistic missile.
  • July 25, 2002: A.P.J. Abdul Kalam is elected as the 11th President of India, becoming the first scientist to hold this position.
  • 2007: After completing his term as the President, Kalam returns to his role as a professor, public speaker, and writer.
  • July 27, 2015: A.P.J. Abdul Kalam passes away due to cardiac arrest while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong.
  • A.P.J. Abdul Kalam's contributions to India's space and missile programs, along with his dedication to education and inspiring the youth, have made him an iconic figure in Indian history.
